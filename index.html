Snapchat is an American multimedia. It is multimedia messaging app. It is developed by Snap Inc., originally launched in 2011. Snapchat has evolved from a messaging app into a social media platform with a focus on visual communication and augmented reality. It is known for its unique feature of sending photos and videos known as "Snaps". Users can send Snaps to friends, which can be viewed for a limited time before disappearing. Messages are usually available for only a short time. There are also options for adding text, drawings, or stickers to the Snaps.

Instagram is a popular social media platform primarily focused on photo and video sharing. Instagram was originally a photo-sharing app that featured several filters and enhancements for photos taken on a mobile phone. Users can share photos or videos on their profile, which remain visible unless deleted. These can include captions, hashtags, and location tags. Users can edit their photos and videos with various filters, effects, and editing tools to enhance their content. Instagram is widely used by individuals, influencers, and businesses for personal branding, marketing, and building communities. Instagram was created by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger, and it was launched on October 6, 2010.


LinkedIn was launched in May 2003 by Reid Hoffman, Allen Blue, Konstantin Guericke, Eric Ly, and Jean-Luc Vaillant. The platform enables professionals to connect, share, and learn, while also serving as a valuable tool for recruiters and job seekers. LinkedIn is a professional networking platform designed for career development, business networking, and job searching. Users create a profile showcasing their professional background, including work experience, education, skills, and certifications. Profiles act as online résumés or CVs. It is widely used in industries such as technology, finance, education, and marketing.
